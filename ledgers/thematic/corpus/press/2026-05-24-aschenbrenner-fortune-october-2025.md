# Fortune — Aschenbrenner's $1.5B AI Hedge Fund: Inner Circle Profile (October 2025)

> Source: WebFetch 2026-05-24 of fortune.com (published October 8, 2025; reporter Sharon Goldman)

## TL;DR

- **The definitive team / inner-circle profile** — first public naming of [[carl-shulman]] as Director of Research, [[graham-duncan]] as advisor + personal LP, [[avital-balwit]] as fiancée, [[sholto-douglas]] as friend / informal thought-partner.
- **AUM at time of writing: $1.5B+** (later 3.7× growth to $5.5B by Feb 2026 per [[2026-05-24-sa-lp-fortune-march-2026]]).
- **H1 2025 returns: 47% net of fees** — first on-record P&L number.
- **Key thesis quote:** *"The decade after is also going to be wild…capital will really matter…there's a lot of money to be made. If AGI were priced in tomorrow, you could maybe make 100x."*
- **Aschenbrenner has "almost all of his net worth invested in the fund"** — confirms full personal alignment.
- **Strategic vehicle choice:** explicitly rejected VC for hedge fund — *"AGI was going to be so impactful to the global economy that the only way to fully capitalize on it was to express investment ideas in the most liquid markets in the world."*

## Key claims — the inner circle

**Aschenbrenner (Founder & PM)** — 23 at writing, Columbia valedictorian at 19, ex-OpenAI Superalignment.

**[[carl-shulman]] — Director of Research.** Age 45 at writing. Previously at [[peter-thiel]]'s [[clarium-capital]] (where he reportedly "translated AI insights into trading strategies"). Long-running AI forecaster, [[80000-hours]] research advisor, ex-FHI / GPI-adjacent. **This is the single most important fact in the article for the subagent design** — SA LP is NOT a one-man shop; Shulman's published intelligence-explosion economics likely shape the portfolio mathematically. See [[2026-05-24-shulman-80k-hours-podcast]] for his canonical published views.

**[[graham-duncan]] — Advisor + personal LP.** Veteran hedge-fund allocator (East Rock Capital co-founder, "manager of managers"). Quoted: *"If you want to have variant perception, it helps to be a little variant."* Cited fund's reaction to the DeepSeek R1 selloff (bought rather than sold) as evidence of Aschenbrenner's credibility. Also offered the load-bearing skeptic line: *"He could yet be proven wrong."*

**Anchor LPs (confirmed in March 2026 Fortune as well):** [[patrick-collison]] + [[john-collison]] (Stripe); [[nat-friedman]] (Meta AI product lead); [[daniel-gross]] (now co-leading Meta Compute with Friedman). Friedman seeded after reading Aschenbrenner's 2023 blog post. Patrick Collison met him at a 2021 dinner.

**Personal network with direct Anthropic information channels:**
- **[[avital-balwit]] — fiancée.** Chief of Staff to [[dario-amodei]] at [[anthropic]]. Ex-FTX Future Fund colleague of Aschenbrenner.
- **[[sholto-douglas]] — friend, former roommate.** Anthropic tech lead on scaling RL. Reportedly a thought-partner on the *Situational Awareness* essays. Quoted: *"He is saying, 'I have an extremely high conviction…I am literally putting my money where my mouth is.'"*

**Organizational structure:** lean — appears to be small portfolio + research shop with dedicated communications rep for media inquiries. (Form ADV per [[situational-awareness-lp]] entity later confirms ~4 employees.)

## Key claims — strategy

- **Long beneficiary stocks** (semiconductors, infrastructure, power) **offset by shorts on lagging industries.** This October 2025 framing precedes the Q1 2026 13F revealing the $8.46B notional put complex — at the time of writing, the put-overlay scale was not yet public.
- **June 2024 13F holdings cited:** Intel (~$459M in call options Q1), Broadcom, Vistra Energy, Core Scientific (later acquired by CoreWeave).
- **DeepSeek R1 reaction (early 2025):** the fund "viewed the selloff as an overreaction and bought positions rather than selling" — Duncan flagged this as evidence of independent thinking.
- **Vehicle-choice rationale:** liquid public markets were chosen explicitly over VC. The fund is positioned to express AGI-thesis trades on a daily basis, not capital-deployment cycles.

## OpenAI-firing detail (incremental)

Aschenbrenner characterized the incident that triggered his firing as sharing *"a brainstorming document on preparedness, safety, and security measures needed in the future on the path to AGI"* with *"three external researchers for feedback — something he said was 'totally normal' at OpenAI at the time."* Reinforces [[2026-05-24-aschenbrenner-dwarkesh-podcast]]'s account; nothing new but corroborates.
