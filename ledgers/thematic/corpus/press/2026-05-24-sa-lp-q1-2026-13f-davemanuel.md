## The strategic shift Q4 2025 → Q1 2026

The Q4 2025 book was constructive on AI infrastructure (power, neoclouds, fuel cells, uranium). Q1 2026 added a **bearish overlay** on first-order chip names.

Aschenbrenner is NOT betting against AGI arrival or against the capex magnitude. He is betting that **public-equity multiples on the chip leaders have run ahead of what 2027-2028 capex returns will support.**

Key implicit messages:
1. **Oracle put significance:** Bearish hedge signals belief that "the most aggressive multiple-expansion in the consensus AI cloud trade has happened in the wrong places."
2. **Valuation compression view:** Not bearish on AGI or capex — bearish on whether chip designers' stocks can outperform from current levels.
3. **Volatility over direction:** Two-way bets on memory (Micron) and foundry (TSMC) = conviction in extreme swings without predicting winners.
4. **Small "starter" commons** in shorted names (e.g. $498K NVDA position alongside $1.57B puts) = optionality preservation, not conviction longs.

## Methodological caveats (Dave Manuel notes)

- All option values reflect **notional underlying value**, NOT premium paid.
- The $13.68B figure mixes full market-value stock holdings with leverage-discounted options positions.
- **Actual cash deployment + maximum loss exposure on puts are materially smaller than notional headlines suggest.**
- 13F shows US equity longs + listed options only. Hidden: shorts, international positions, private investments, currencies, bonds.
