## TL;DR

- **Background reading on [[carl-shulman]], Director of Research at [[situational-awareness-lp]]** — the most detailed public articulation of his views on intelligence-explosion economics, which functionally underpin SA LP's positioning.
- Shulman is the *quantitative-economist* counterpart to Aschenbrenner's *strategic-doctrine* writing. Where Aschenbrenner reasons in OOMs and scenarios, Shulman reasons in watts, brain-equivalents, and doubling times.
- **Key quantitative frame:** per-person energy budgets could sustain *"50,000 human brain equivalents of AI cognitive labour, 10,000 human-scale robots"* if solar and computational technology match biological efficiency (~20W per brain).
- **Bottleneck identification matches SA LP positioning:** Shulman emphasizes **heat dissipation as the ultimate physical constraint**; power generation and infrastructure are binding constraints long before AI capability is. This is the theoretical underpinning of the long-power + long-data-center thesis in SA LP's Q1 2026 13F per [[2026-05-24-sa-lp-q1-2026-13f-davemanuel]].

## Key claims

**Intelligence-explosion economics:**
- AI matching human brain efficiency (20W per brain) could enable **explosive economic growth**. Per-person energy budgets are sufficient for ~50,000 brain-equivalents of cognitive labor.
- **Economic doubling times could shrink to months** rather than years, with growth bottlenecked by *resource availability* rather than labor.
- Biological precedents (cyanobacteria doubling in 12 hours) demonstrate the physical feasibility of fast-doubling self-replicating systems.

**Power as the binding constraint:**
- *"If you kept adding more, it would raise the temperature too much."*
- Heat dissipation forces eventual expansion beyond Earth — i.e. terrestrial power generation is the near-term limit.
- This is the **direct economic-physics justification** for SA LP's long-power-+-data-infrastructure positioning.

**Capital deployment:**
- Skeptical of economist-consensus dismissing explosive-growth scenarios (echoes Aschenbrenner's "few hundred people have situational awareness" framing).
- Concerned about geopolitical instability from uneven AI deployment across nations (consistent with Aschenbrenner's CCP-threat framing in [[2026-05-24-aschenbrenner-sa-iiid-free-world-must-prevail]]).

**Moral / coexistence concerns:**
- Addresses moral status of artificial minds as a coexistence challenge (distinct from Aschenbrenner's national-security-only framing).
